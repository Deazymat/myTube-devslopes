There's a certain humorous delight in watching League of Legends players make their first foray into DOTA 2. The transition isn't always smooth, but it is certainly entertaining. It's like watching a veteran swimmer try to navigate the waters with a completely different set of rules and physics. They're in the same general environment — the familiar world of MOBAs — but the experience is a universe apart. A League player might find themselves scrambling as they face DOTA 2's vast hero pool, along with the absence of their cherished Summoner Spells. Seeing them click furiously to move, forgetting they can simply right click, or watching their bewildered reactions when they lose gold upon death, is a sight to behold. Some may even have the initial reflex to look for the recall button, only to find that teleporting back to base now requires a purchasable item. The nuances in map control, the strategic importance of the elevated terrain, the micro-management of the courier, the layered mechanics of denying creeps — these are but a few of the elements that add depth to DOTA 2 and can baffle an unprepared League of Legends player. But the humor doesn't stem from mockery. Instead, it's a gentle, shared mirth at the universal experience of stepping outside of one's comfort zone. After all, the thrill of discovering and mastering a new game is part of why we play. The stumbles, the confusion, the moment of revelation when things finally start to click - all of these make the transition from League to DOTA a comedy of errors worth tuning into.
